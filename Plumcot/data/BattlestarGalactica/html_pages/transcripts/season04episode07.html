ACT ONE

Colonial One

(Laura listens to a tape on a reel to reel with her eyes closed, as Lee watches.)

Baltar, on tape: "Now I have learned from unimpeachable sources that President Laura Roslin has for some time now been sharing hallucinogenic visions with two Cylons within our Fleet. One Sharon Agathon, sent on the classified Demetrius mission, and the other a Cylon prisoner being held aboard the Galac..."

(Laura turns it off, finally.)

Apollo: Uh-uh. It's not that easy, Madam President.

Roslin: No? You should try it sometime.

Apollo: Most of the population has heard that broadcast.

Roslin: I would assume that's the point.

Apollo: Look, I take no pleasure in putting this before you. In fact, they practically had to push me through that door....

Roslin: Well, I'm sure you're quite happy to stay.

Apollo: I can't put one foot in front of another without someone blocking my path asking me what the hell is going on! For the welfare of the Fleet...

Roslin: -- Excuse me. As long as I am in this office, the welfare of this Fleet is not something you need to worry about.

Apollo: You owe the people a response.

Roslin, scoffing: I owe the people?

Apollo: That's what I believe.

Roslin: What if suddenly all your beliefs were called into question?

Apollo: Madam President...

Roslin: -- Up is down. Black is white. Scripture is fiction. Home is thin air, instead of solid ground. Et cetera.

Apollo: You know, I'm really sorry for everything you've had to endure.

(Laura laughs and shakes her head.)

Apollo: I really am. But the people, they deserve a response.

Roslin: Do they?

Apollo: Is there any truth to this broadcast?

Roslin: And what would you do if I told you the truth? What if it were you instead of me, and all that's left of humanity has just been told that you are sharing visions with the enemy? What would you do, Mr. Adama? Would you think that the Gods were testing you?

Apollo: So Baltar is telling the truth?

(Laura looks at him without answering.)

Apollo, leaning forward: Is it true?

Roslin: ...Yes.

Rebel Basestar

(Kara, Natalie, the Eight and Leoben wait while Athena works in the Raptor.)

Athena: Demetrius reports their sys ops will be synced with ours in five minutes.

(Natalie is afraid.)

(Natalie and Leoben look at each other: Natalie will lead the rebels in negotiations.)

Starbuck: Good. Gods help us.

Starbuck: Good. Tell the Demetrius to set the board to green. Unless both ships jump in together, Galactica will blow us out of the sky.

Dualla: Dradis contact!

Dualla: Demetrius, Galactica. Authenticate.

ACT TWO

(Raptors scramble to the Baseship and Demetrius.)

Rebel Basestar

Tigh, leading a Marine squad: Move, move, move!

(As Tigh and the Marines enter, Kara stands at the head of the group.).

Starbuck: Colonel, they're with me.

Tigh: They're with you?

(Saul stares at them: Natalie, the Eight, Athena, Sam, Kara.)

Tigh: So which one of them shot Gaeta?

Galactica Sickbay

Cottle: Lieutenant Gaeta, stay with us. Elevate the leg. Prep an IV. All right, everybody. Stand back. And get a cut down tray in here right now.

Galactica: Adama's Offices

(Natalie sits before Tory and Tigh, the President and the Admiral; a squad of Marines watches.)

Natalie: we became divided according to models. The Twos, Eights, and Sixes have come to believe that our destiny lies in seeking out the Final Five. The Ones, Fours, and Fives violently opposed such action.

Adama: We know that there are seven different models of Cylons. You only mentioned six.

Athena: The Threes. The D'Annas? They were boxed after the battle on the Algae Planet.

Tigh: Boxed?

Natalie: Her entire line was punitively deactivated. Her consciousness placed in a boxing facility.

Adama: Why?

Natalie: D'Anna saw the faces of the Final Five, which was forbidden. But if we unbox her, she can reveal their identities to us.

Starbuck: The Final Five have been to Earth. If we help find them, they can help us get there.

Natalie: Our ship can heal itself. But most of our Raiders were destroyed in the ambush. We're asking for your help here. We can't do it alone.

Roslin: What could possibly motivate us to trust you now? What have you got?

Natalie: The boxing facility resides within the Cylon Resurrection Hub. This Hub controls the functions of every Resurrection Ship in existence.

Athena: It protects itself by periodically jumping to a new set of coordinates and then relaying them back to the Baseship.

Natalie, standing as the Marines take aim on her: Madam President, you asked for a reason to help us? Vengeance. You destroy the Hub, Cylons lose their ability to download. All of us.

Roslin: Why would you be willing to lose your ability to resurrect?

Natalie, sadly: We're rebels. We can't go back. What matters most to us is being with the Five. D'Anna will be able to identify them. We'll take you to the Hub if you help us unbox D'Anna.

Adama: Give us the coordinates of the Hub first.

Natalie: No, I won't do that. I said we would take you there.

Adama: Take her out. We're finished.

Natalie, at the door: Wait. Is your word good, Admiral? I'll give you the coordinates. But I want your word.

Adama: Stand down. You have my word.

Natalie: Once their identities are revealed and we come back here, we'll be free to leave your Fleet with the Five?

(Laura is surprised.)

Natalie: Yes, they're here with you. The Final Five are in your Fleet. That's why our Raiders turned back the attack in the Nebula.

(Tory and Saul stare and act weird.)

Galactica Corridor

Adama: The weapons hold. How the frak did you see that?

Tigh: To be absolutely honest with you, Bill... I didn't. What can I say? We got lucky.

Adama: Well, let's get a Raptor up to those coordinates that that Cylon gave us. Maybe we can get lucky again.

Tigh, leaving: Sir.

Adama: Tigh? Thank you.

(Saul nods.)

Resurrection Hub

(Racetrack and Skulls stare from their Raptor.)

Racetrack: Lords of Kobol, shield your eyes. Get it with the gun camera. Hit override.

Skulls: Kinda pretty, isn't it?

Racetrack: Tell it to the Fleet.

(They jump back.)

Galactica Sickbay

(Cottle prepares for Felix's surgery.)

Gaeta: No. No. Don't put me under. Please. Just... Just numb everything.

Cottle: Felix.

Gaeta: Just do it, I... I don't want to wake up... I don't want to wake up with my leg gone.

Cottle: Okay, son.

(Anders watches as Cottle marks the incision; Cottle starts up a bone saw; Anders looks away and Felix closes his eyes, terrified.)

ACT THREE

(The Fleet jump back to Galactica and the Basestar.)

Galactica: Adama's Offices

Helo: If we go in on our own horse, we won't last five minutes. But we go in with that Baseship parked out there, nobody's gonna notice us for awhile.

Tigh: What do we fight with? Their Raiders are frakked.

Helo: We don't use theirs. We use ours. Take half the Viper wing.

Adama: Half the wing?

Helo: Yeah. We'll sneak them in on the rebel Baseship. Hoshi did some calculations from Racetrack's photos. We take out the FTL and the Hub's stranded.

Tigh: We blow the Hub and, uh, billions of skin jobs lose their bath privileges.

Roslin: Imagine. Once they're gone, they can't come back. Mortal enemies.

Helo: Before we blow the Hub, we need to get a team in to unbox D'Anna.

Tigh: Well, now, maybe we just don't. Maybe we just forget the Godsdamn boxing facility.

Adama: Lie to the rebels?

Tigh: Well, they're lying to us. The skin jobs said their Raiders won't fight because the Final Five is here? We tangled with those slit-eyed black bastards for three years now, and they haven't turned back before. Why now?

Helo: What if they're telling the truth? What if the Final Five are here?

Adama: The Five could know the way to Earth.

Roslin: If they do, they're not gonna lead us there after we've just destroyed their resurrection capacity.

Adama: Maybe they're different, like the rebels.

Roslin: What if they're not? What if their sole purpose is to destroy us?

Tigh: I say we blow the whole Godsdamn thing to dust, lock, stock, and barrel, and trust the President's vision. Her scriptures, her way to Earth.

Roslin: Why don't we split the difference? Unbox D'Anna and find the Five. Keep the Five till we get to Earth, turn them over to the rebels. They've waited this long. It'll be on our terms. ...It's all pie in the sky, gentlemen, until we know what's true and what's not.

Adama: And we put a lid on it. The real plan stays in this room. If the plan gets leaked to the Cylons, all bets are off. Have someone escort the rebel leader back to her Baseship so she can inform her people they have a deal.

(Five Raptors land on the Baseship; the three rebels stand in the middle of the Viper wing as Colonial deckhands swarm their deck.)

Colonial One: Quorum Chambers

Zarek, reading: "In thirty-six hours, the Colonies will unite forces with insurgent rebel Cylons and their leader in an unprecedented joint military operation to destroy the Cylons' ability to download into... " Blah blah blah. Why weren't we consulted?

(The Delegates stare at Lee.)

Apollo: I'm sorry. I'm in the dark as much as all of you...

Galactica: Tool Room

Chief: You know, if they unbox the D'Annas, at least we'll find out who the fifth one is.

Tigh: All that's gonna do is crowd the airlock a little more. Do you think they're gonna give you a medal when they find out who you are?

Tory: what about you?

Anders: He sings, you know.

Chief: ...What?

Anders: Gaeta. Whenever he feels the tingling, whenever he feels his phantom leg. Cottle says it helps him get through it. He sings.

Colonial One: President's Offices

Tory, entering: Good morning.

Roslin: Morning. What is it about the Galactica that gives you such a glow? I come over here, it's like I'm going from one dungeon to the next.

Tory: Oh, I hadn't noticed. But thank you.

Roslin: You ready?

Tory: Yes.

Roslin: I want you to find out who's behind the shared vision rumors. Who's talking to him?

Tory: With Baltar?

Roslin: You're sleeping with him, right?

(Tory stares at her, afraid; she opens her mouth.)

Roslin: Don't.

(Tory looks at her guiltily.)

Roslin: I've just been informed that you've been spotted down there enough times to be a charter member of his nymph squad.

Tory, beginning to cry: All right. I have come to believe in Baltar's spiritual message. I don't know how or why. It just happened. I wish you knew how many times I wanted to tell you. Your friendship and your trust means...

Roslin: -- Frak. Clearly my friendship and trust mean frak. And I don't really care if you have to spend the night on your knees praying, or just on your knees. I want a name. I want to know who's responsible for these lies.

(Laura turns her back on Tory and puts on her glasses.)

Tory: Madam President. Laura. I am so sorry.

Roslin, without turning: You have a job to do.

(Tory leaves.)

Rebel Basestar

(The three rebels speak quietly, under Marine watch.)

Natalie: The humans are never going to allow us to have the Final Five and go our own way.

Eight: Are you sure of this?

Natalie: Not entirely. I'd rather be wrong. But we need insurance.

Leoben: Trust has to begin somewhere.

Natalie: Not with us. It's our ship. When we jump, we take control of it with the Centurions. We carry out the mission as promised. But when we return, we take hostages. The humans on this ship stay on this ship until we have the Five.

(Leoben looks away, distraught.)

Natalie: Look at me. We've changed, but the humans haven't. We've come too far to risk everything on their trust.

(All three reluctantly agree.)

Galactica Sickbay

(The other patients, including Laura, listen as Felix sings.)

Gaeta: "Alone she sleeps, in the shirt of man / With my three wishes clutched in her hand / The first that she be spared the pain / That comes from a dark and laughing rain / When she finds love, may it always stay true ..."

(Lee enters Roslin's bedsit, listening to Felix.)

Roslin: What a way to discover such a beautiful voice, huh? How'd you find me?

Apollo: Your Chief of Staff.

Roslin: ...Needs to be horsewhipped. If you came down here to kiss my ass to get information, save your breath.

Apollo: The Quorum's considering a vote of no confidence against you.

(Gaeta's voice gets louder.)

Roslin: I won't compromise the success of this operation or the safety of this Fleet to indulge the neediness of twelve perpetually unhappy representatives. I can't.

(Gaeta gets even louder, then goes quiet.)

Apollo: I was with them on the, uh, emergency jump. And I saw something in the faces of the Delegates. It wasn't anxiety from waiting, it went beyond that. I was anxious, it was my first time, but they... They were empty. The Quorum had given up. And then it struck me that after going through the same routine so many times, it was the only way that they could cope with the uncertainty. To presume the worst.

Roslin, softly: You felt their suffering. Now try holding their lives in balance every day.

Apollo: Talk to them. You don't have to tell them everything, I know how it works. Just talk to the Quorum. Let them put a face to this joint mission, let them hear from the Cylon leader, anything. Anything to put their fears to rest.

ACT FOUR

(Gaius and Tory lie in bed; she won't look at him.)

Baltar: What's the matter?

Tory: Nothing.

Baltar: No, nothing. Nothing at all. We've been lying here doing nothing. What?

Tory: You've been lying about the President sharing visions with Cylons.

Baltar, sitting up: That happens to be the God's honest truth.

Tory, nearly weeping: Sorry. I can't accept what you've done.

Baltar: What... What I've done? What have I...? What are... Where's this coming from?

Tory: These rumors you've started. I've seen what they've done to her.

Baltar: You seriously believe that I'm making all this up? I am not saying I'm not capable, but why would I? Six revealed... Caprica Six revealed them to me, through my attorney in the last days of my trial, when a guilty verdict was a certainty. I suppose I could've gone public. Caused a stir...

Tory: And all this time you kept quiet?

Baltar: I'm not saying I'm a saint, Tory. I'm many things, but I try not to be spiteful. Got no bloodlust to go get her. But she's a hypocrite. The lies, the secret missions. Co-opting the rhetoric of patriotism to keep everyone in the dark, including you. That's rather worrying, isn't it? So I had to speak. The truth is, no good ever comes from concealing it.

(Gaius rises to dress.)

Baltar: I've got a broadcast.

Galactica Sickbay

Gaeta, quietly: "The first that she be spared the pain / That comes from a dark and laughing rain ..."

(Felix swallows, nearly cries; the camera pans down his body to his leg.)

Gaeta: "When she finds love may it always stay true / This I beg for the second wish I made too ..."

Colonial One: Quorum Chambers

Roslin, weakly: I didn't come here to beg, or plead, or apologize, or to keep my job. You know me better than that. And I didn't come here to create sympathy by parading my illness in front of you. On the contrary, there are some that probably believe that along with my cancer drugs, I have inoculated myself against compassion.

(She smiles, they are quiet.)

Roslin: That is a joke.

(Silence.)

Roslin: ...I am here because I want to tell you face to face that I believe in this mission and what it means for our future. I'm here to profess my trust in our new allies. I'm here to ask you to listen, I'm here to ask for your support. Bring her in.

(The Marines bring Natalie in, uncuffed; the Quorum stares at her hatefully. Kara and Athena join them. Natalie stands at the head of the room, with Laura.)

Natalie: In our civil war, we've seen death. We've watched our people die. Gone forever. As terrible as it was, beyond the reach of the Resurrection Ships, something began to change. We could feel a sense of time, as if each moment held its own significance. We began to realize that for our existence to hold any value, it must end. To live meaningful lives, we must die and not return. The one human flaw that you spend your lifetimes distressing over, mortality, is the one thing... Well, it's the one thing that makes you whole. I believe it was no accident that we were found by Kara Thrace. It was destiny...

(Kara starts to fugue out.)

Natalie: She asked me to lead my people, and I accepted. No matter what the sacrifice, even if it should mean my death...

Hybrid, in Kara's memory: Thus shall it come to pass. The dying leader shall know the truth of the Opera House. You are the harbinger of death, Kara Thrace.

Natalie: ...Our destiny, our future, begins here. Thank you for giving me the opportunity to speak.

(Natalie and Laura nod to each other.)

Adama: Take her out.

Galactica Sickbay

Gaeta: "But wish no more / My life you can take / To have her please just one day wake / To have her please ..."

(Falsetto; he cries out and goes quiet. Gaius watches him, nearly weeping.)

Gaeta: "Alone she sleeps, in the shirt of man / With my three wishes clutched in her hand ..."

Galactica/Kobol Opera House

(Laura and Athena search for Hera.)

Roslin: Hera!

Athena: Hera! Hera.

(Caprica dreams in the brig, crying; she takes the child in her arms and enters the auditorium with Gaius. Laura wakes, gasping. The doors close in front of Athena and she wakes -- Hera is standing by her bedside.)

Hera: Bye-bye!

ACT FIVE

Galactica Sickbay

(Kara watches Laura read; she notices, and smiles at her.)

Roslin: I gotta hand it to you. If you are a Cylon, that was a great plan. Dangle yet another way to Earth, throw in the Hub, the Final Five, and the real kicker, put the Final Five on the Fleet. Even I couldn't pass that one up.

Starbuck: You are having those visions, aren't you?

(Laura looks back down at her book; Kara enters.)

Starbuck: "Thus shall it come to pass. The dying leader shall know the truth of the Opera House..."

Roslin: -- What did you say?

Starbuck: "Thus shall it come to pass..."

Roslin: The Opera House.

Starbuck: "The dying leader shall know the truth of the Opera House."

Roslin, whispering: Where did... Where did you hear that? Who told you that?

Starbuck: The Hybrid on the Baseship, before they unplugged it.

Roslin: The Hybrid... How does the Hybrid know what's in my dream?

Starbuck: I wish I knew.

Roslin: This has got to stop. These visions, I've got to find out about these visions. I've got to know. Will you help me?

Starbuck: Yes.

Roslin: Find Captain Agathon.

Rebel Basestar

Natalie: When I spoke to the Quorum, I could feel it. The contempt.

Leoben: You were right. They're not ready to trust us.

Natalie: No, I was wrong. We're not ready. We're deceiving them.

Eight: For protection. For necessity.

Natalie: Out of suspicion and fear. Why haven't the Final Five come forward? What if they're watching, judging us on our actions? We're about to resort to violence and coercion. What if they refuse to come with us? We can't do this.

Leoben: It's too late.

Natalie: We have to order the Centurions to stand down.

Leoben, standing: It's too late. They're committed.

Natalie: Then we have to tell the humans the truth.

Eight, horrified: No, you can't do that. They'll never trust us again. They already know the coordinates. They'll just go destroy everything.

Leoben: Go. Stall for time. Talk to Adama. Tell him anything. Anything but the truth. I'll deal with the Centurions.

Galactica Hangar Bay

(Helo and Marines march Gaius onto the deck; he whispers something to Kara as Baltar boards a Raptor. He sees Laura and nearly stumbles.)

Baltar: Of course.

Roslin, with her copy of Searider Falcon in her lap: Have a seat. We're going to settle this now.

Baltar: What's to settle? Your Chief of Staff is ... incomparably talented.

Roslin: I've been sharing visions with Sharon Agathon and the Six.

Baltar: So I was only speaking the truth.

Roslin: Yes. The Hybrid on the Baseship spoke of an Opera House and a dying leader. We're going to talk to the Hybrid.

Baltar: And why would you require my presence?

Roslin: Because you're in my visions.

Galactica: Agathon Quarters

(Hera's drawing with her crayons as Athena enters, pats her head, drops her gear, and sits on the bed.)

Athena: What are you doing, baby? Are you drawing? Can I see what you drew?

(Hera smiles and hands it over: a book of drawings of the numeral 6, and identical blondes holding hands. Most of them are wearing red.)

Athena: Oh, no. No! No! No! Why? Why? Why?

(Hera has disappeared into the corridors; as Athena searches, her vision of the Opera House coincides with Galactica's halls.)

Athena: Hera? Hera? Hera!

(Kara, Helo, his Marines, Laura and Gaius disembark a Raptor on the Basestar; Viper pilots stare as a Centurion walks across the deck.)

(Natalie rides to Galactica on a Raptor.)

Ensign: "Raptor 124, you are cleared for landing..."

(They land; Tigh conducts her through the corridors toward Adama.)

(Athena searches Galactica/the Opera House.)

Athena: Hera!

(Hera laughs.)

Athena: Hera. Hera! Have you seen my daughter?

Dualla: No, I haven't.

Athena: Hera! Hera!

(Natalie is marched through Galactica; Laura and Gaius make their way toward the Hybrid.)

Athena: Hera! Hera! Hera!

(The steps of the Galactica become the stair of the Opera House; Athena sees Galen doing scut work.)

Athena: I can't find Hera...

(Concerned, he turns off his soldering iron and follows her.)

(The Colonials come closer to the Hybrid's Chamber.)

(Gaeta's Lament begins to play as Hera reaches Natalie in the corridor; Natalie smiles and kneels to touch her, embracing her; Athena comes around the corner and sees Caprica lifting Hera into her arms in the Opera House.)

Athena, pulling a gun: Hera! Get away from my child. Get your hands off of my child.

Tigh: Athena...

(The Marines level their weapons at her.)

Athena: Stay back! Stay away.

Tigh: Stand down!

(Laura reaches the Hybrid and kneels at her side, staring down at her.)
